Making the organization more humane and engaging

Google supports employees’ families. It offers a generous death benefit that pays a deceased employee’s spouse or partner 50% of their salary for 10 years.

Learn more

Petsmart offers its employees a variety of pet-related benefits such as a 15% discount on pet-related merchandise and offers free training classes and discounted veterinary visits for the pets of their employees.

Learn more

Range has a #coffeecooler channel, a clever play on the word water cooler conversations through which employees can find common ground with co-workers who share similar interests and humour, which makes the employee connect with their colleagues in a better way.

Learn more

Smule lets its employees bring their instruments to the office for the company’s weekly jam sessions. There are also various holiday parties, including a Halloween costume party, Thanksgiving lunch, Wednesday game nights and monthly karaoke party.

Learn more

Addigy, a software company offers a method to stop boring meetings. If someone feels that a meeting is going off track or is becoming boring, they are allowed to throw in a physical yellow flag or show a virtual yellow flag over the video call, which will bring attention to the individual so that the group can hear them out.

Learn more

Alloy, a fintech firm covers 50 percent of legal expenses, up to $5,000, for anyone navigating newly enacted anti-abortion laws in the US. The company also offers up to $1,500 for travel expenses and another $1,500 for additional out-of-pocket costs for the expenses that will be borne because employees may want to travel out for working around the policy.

Learn more

Aqueduct Technologies, an IT firm, the Head of HR, has an one-on-one session with its employees where the employees speak about not only their own roles, but also the organization as a whole and how it can improve. The idea: Why wait until it's too late to find out hard truths about your company? This has helped the company have an engaged set of employees by solving most of its problems beforehand.

Learn more

BambooHR has a novel, nonthreatening way of handling mistakes. It created an Oops Email Box as a place for all employees — founders included — to announce mistakes made, what others should be aware of because of them, and steps taken to correct them.

Learn more

Bobbie as BusinessWire mentions, understands that all employees – working parents or not – carry the invisible weight of a mental load and neverending to-do list. In an effort to alleviate that load, the company offers a unique, dedicated Balance Coordinator to support all team members with tasks completely unrelated to work, like finding a weekend babysitter or dog walker.

Learn more

Bobbie as BusinessWire mentions, celebrates all pregnancy announcements, empathizes with pregnancy loss with a miscarriage leave, and has embraced being 100% remote with schedule flexibility for parents.

Learn more

L’Oréal knows that one of the best way to engage with a new employee is before they join and soon after they join. This is when they also require the maximum support. As part of their global onboarding programme for all newcomers, L’Oréal has developed an employee onboarding app with the sole purpose of assisting newcomers to understand, decode and master their company culture. The app, which is called ‘Fit’, reaches up to 10,000 new hires per year and is available in 11 languages, helping new recruits navigate the L’Oréal culture and complete real-life “missions” in order to put their learning into practice.

Learn more

Microsoft found out that that when its new hires met with their buddy more than eight times in their first 90 days on the job, 97% said that their buddy helped them become productive quickly. This has prompted them to focus on their buddy program and make it mandatory for all new joiners to connect with their buddies on a regular basis.

Learn more

MB Group is an accounting firm known for promoting work-life balance for its employees. The owners Bryant and Janet Haston Quinones bought out the last male partner at their firm and set about reshaping the MB Group's work culture to be pro-family, pro-balance, and supportive of employees' growth. All the policies and practices in the firm are created with a focus towards creating a better workplace. As part of this, they got rid of its process for tracking billable hours and sent a strong signal that it values employees and the work they do.

Learn more

Patagonia as Zavvy mentions in its blog, believes in various ways to build a positive work culture and drive employee engagement. For example, the company offers an in-house child care solution for parents who need it and surf time for employees who want to take advantage of good wave conditions when they are thriving.

Learn more

Seer Interactive has placed stacks of unbranded thank-you cards with envelopes, pens, and stamps near every elevator in its office. As Inc.com says, it is not uncommon to see employees at the 19-year-old company scribble a note while they're waiting or grab a few on their way home to acknowledge a colleague or someone in their life. Free stationery may seem trivial, but it's an analog sign of the digital marketing agency's intention to cultivate a culture of gratitude. And it's very on-brand.

Seer Interactive gave $1500 to each of it 220 employees as soon as the COVID-19 pandemic hit as part of its efforts to support its employees.
