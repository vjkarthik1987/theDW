Managing compensation

Netflix has a combination of stock options and salary as part of its regular compensation package. Each employee can choose to go for all cash, all stock, or a combination that suits them based on their risk appetite. These 10 year stock options are fully vested so that an employee can keep them even if they leave Netflix.

Learn more

Netflixpays its employees at the top of their personal market. Netflix does not think of these as 'raises' as there is no raise pool to divide and distribute. This is done purely based on market adjustment and this does not wait for annual compensation changes.

Learn more

Seer Interactive raised its minimum salary to $65,000 several years back when the standard minimum wage was much lower. While its CEO and the company itself was ridiculed for this step, the company's success in retaining its employees has proved that the decision was right.

Learn more

Semler Corp, three decades ago, let its employees put a computer in the company cafeteria that revealed how much revenue the company was taking in, the profit margins from that revenue, how much employees inside the company made, and how much employees in similar positions made. Armed with that information, the employee was then allowed to set his or her own salary. Did people pay themselves ridiculous amounts? No, it turned out that information and peer pressure kept pay at industry norms.

Learn more

u2i pays its employees a share of the profit that the organization gets. As Corporate Rebels point out, they first implemented an early version of their profit sharing for senior managers in 2008. After initial feedback, the next level was included in the profit sharing mechanism in 2010. And today, every employee receives a share of the profit in an amount that is proportionate to their salary.

Gravity Payments has raised its minimum wage to $70,000 - more than 40% compared to its competitors. This has ensured that the turnout of its workforce is below the industry average.
