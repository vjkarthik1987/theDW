Giving Benefits and Perks

Era Software, a data management company provides a $3,000 home-office stipend during an employee's first year. Workers get another $1,500 each year for additional upgrades.

Learn more

Fusion Medical Staffing a health care staffing company offers its employees access to a full-time handyman to help with everything from broken garage doors to bathroom tile work. This allows workers to address nagging repairs without having to wait around at home for Mr. Fix It.

Learn more

Penguin Random House offers free books to its employees. They can order multiple books and e-books, free of charge, from a list of more than 100 titles, which is updated throughout the year. Employees can also join or form book clubs with their colleagues to discuss plots and practice team building.

Learn more

Salesforce, through its Wellness Reimbursement Program offers staff $100 each month to use on any wellness activity they like – Yoga classes, nutrition therapy, and more.

Learn more

Starbucks offers its employees, which it calls as partners, a Spotify subscription. All employees are eligible on hire.

Learn more

Atradius an insurance provider has awarded its 426 members of staff a £1,600 payment, in order to support them during the cost-of-living crisis.

Learn more

Stealth Venture Labs offers a professional coach to help new and expectant parents manage life with a newborn. The coach assists with preparation for maternity and paternity leave and navigating the transition to becoming working parents.

Learn more

Stella Rising offers a $500 happiness allowance every year for employees to use on whatever brings them joy, such as ski passes or a meal or even a massage
