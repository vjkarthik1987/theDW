Creating a flexible workplace

Chili Piper hosts an annual company offsite to gather employees at some dreamy destination, like Tulum or Ibiza. The relationships spawned from those experiences lead employees, who live across 40 countries, to visit co-workers on their own accord and post photos of themselves in a Slack channel called #whereschilipiper. And the company pays for this visit.

Cisco employees have lots of choice and flexibility when deciding where and when they work. Many people at Cisco work completely remotely, and around 800 people work only 4 days each week, putting in a total of 32 hours.

Front a customer communication software company has a Friday-off policy where its employees can focus on attending customer complaints rather than attend internal calls. It is OK for employees to be unreachable, essentially, on Fridays, with some exceptions. Employees still work, but they don't have to respond to internal emails or calls, and there are no internal meetings. Customers are the only priority.

Hyatt has a policy which allows housekeepers to leave if they complete their tasks on time or clean additional rooms to make more money. Also, its family assistance leave offers a minimum of eight weeks of paid leave to primary caregivers.

Yousician is a Finnish interactive and educational music service designed to help people learn and play musical instruments. As the blog of Zavvy says, in July 2014, Yousician took its entire team to Saarenmaa, Estonia, for some team bonding and to spend time discussing how to improve its product. Suddenly, the company's leadership dawned that they could work anywhere as long as they had their computers. Therefore, when the company discovered their office in Helsinki would undergo a one-month renovation, they decided to get creative.
