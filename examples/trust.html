Trusting the employee

Hubspot believes so much in 'Transparency' and 'Trusting the employee' that it shares all its data (whatever can be shared) with its entire set of employees. They do not hold back on anything - whether it is financial data, or minutes from the leadership meeting. They believe that more eyes can drive a greater insight.

Hyatt, the global hospitality chain does not mandate its employees to follow a script when it comes to talking to its customers. They are free to be their true selves. This ensures that there is a genuine and authentic customer experience and also encourages employees to be sincere and be themselves. In short, Hyatt trusts its employees to do the best thing in the interests of Hyatt.

Netflix has a 5 word policy for travel, entertainment, gifts, and other expenses - Act in Netflix's best interests. They use their guiding principles of context, not control to let their employees make the right judgement to take the right decisions which are effective and is not in conflict with the goals of Netflix. The employees are free to ask if in doubt.

Southwest Airlines is different from other airlines because it lets its employees bring in a degree of individuality and creativity to the jobs they do, even if it is routine. For example, the video of a Southwest flight attendant following a dance routine to make the safey announcement went viral, and it was possible because the airlines did not mandate a routine to hamper the creativity and individuality, as long as the outcomes were met.

Southwest Airlines, keeping up with is tradition of letting its employees take decisions that impact them also did something that was seen as unique in its times. They let their workers decide key aspects of their jobs, right from desiging their uniforms in various departments to giving them autonomy over their work-life and career. This has also been noticed by the customers as they see genuinely happy employees.

Supercell, a game development company lets its developers have full control over what they do. Its CEO encourages the team to take its own decisions even if it may affect the entire company. And the team is supposed to let the company know once the decision has been made. For example, in Supercell, a team was working on a gama called Smash Land, which was supposed to be a game changer. But, sadly the team working on it could not meet the targets, and over a sauna session, the team decided to pull the plug, and the CEO was informed after the decision was made.

u2i, a technology company, has a unique policy in place where there is only one rule for any person attending an event - it is unlimited as long as it is reasonable. The company pays for the accomodation, flight, and all other related expenses, without asking any questions. They even have a slack channel called #expenses where any person attending an event is expected to post with reasons for attending the event and the other details. If anyone has an objection, they can raise it and there is a space for discussion. But, till the time of writing this in this blog, no one has ever been blocked.

u2i has a company wallet which can be used by anyone in the company. If someone is going out for lunch with their team, they can take their company wallet and pay for the lunch. The only rule - if you take out the company wallet, spend the money inside it as if it were your own.

All Web Technologies, a software development company shares all the information related to its business - financial information, or information regarding how the company is doing - to all its employees. As its CEO say, "At AWL, transparency means there are no secrets when it comes to how we run our business, how our business is performing, and how each of us, as individuals, are doing versus our team and personal targets."
