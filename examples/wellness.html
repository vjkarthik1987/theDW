Focusing on employee wellness

GadellNet Consulting Services offers its employees free sessions with a business coach to help with work-life balance. Some of the coaches also hold a clinical therapy license, and all are certified by the International Coaching Federation.

Learn more

Guadalupe, as soon as the pandemic struck, was quick to launch remote classes, and the Fahertys saw this as an opportunity to provide their employees with a way to battle quarantine-induced loneliness and stress. The optional workouts include everything from yoga to cardio, and the founders say they block out time for the sweat sessions during the workday so that their employees feel empowered to prioritize fitness.

Learn more

Patagonia encourages its employees to stay physically active. The California-based company provides bikes, volleyball courts and onsite yoga for its workers.

Learn more

Patagonia in a unique take on the concept of flexible hours, provides surf equipment, posts daily surf reports and makes companywide announcements on especially good surf days this encouraging its employees to catch a wave or two.

Learn more

Steelcase in addition to standard health benefits, takes care of its employees’ health at the office. The design and manufacturing company has an onsite well-being hub where employees can make appointments with a massage therapist or registered nurse.

Learn more

Steelcase offers “healthier home meal” ingredient stations in its cafeterias where employees can prep a meal to cook at home instead of visiting a drive-through after work.

Learn more

Steelcase hosts Curious Minds, a series of lectures on topics like personal wellness and eldercare.
