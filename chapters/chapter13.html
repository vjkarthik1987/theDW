Chapter 13: The Big C Word - Culture

Previous
Next

Culture by definition is the ideas, customs, and behaviors of a group of people.

Let us get this straight - we have a culture, but we don’t go around building a culture. We believe that culture happens and is not created. Every organization has a culture. Even if they do not actively take action and make decisions to create a culture. some emphasize creativity, some emphasize discipline, and some foster innovation. There is no right or wrong answer when it comes to culture. It is relative.

We believe that the people who work with us are responsible adults (most of the time) and behave judiciously (well, most of the time). And hence, they do not need to be educated on how to work and interact.

Our beliefs guide us and we hope that our employees practice that while they work with us. But the foundations of this set of beliefs are trust and accountability. TDW trusts you to be accountable, and you can trust TDW to care for you. And we will let our culture evolve. Like a tree that grows up and a root that spreads out. Cliche alert - Culture is not a destination, but a journey.
