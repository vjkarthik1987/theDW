Chapter 12: We Care for Everyone

Previous
Next

We have a lot of people working with us - across nationalities, across genders, across several ethnic profiles. Our people speak several languages, and are probably working from several countries and several timezones. We do not differentiate and every one is equal for us - irrespective of seniority and irrespective of roles.

For example, all of us, while travelling, stay in the same hotel and have the same kind of room. We do not differentiate.

We also want you to care about everyone and always follow certain basic rules in your day-to-day interactions with your colleagues

  • Be careful, considerate, and conscious - in your behaviour, and in your communication

  • Respect others - everytime and all the time

  • Be ready to accept plurality of views

  • Criticise constructively, and receive feedback with an open heart

We also do not encourage any forms of sexual references or unwelcome sexual advances or any form of violence within our workplace. We also do not encourage any form of discussion based on (as Basecamp puts it) race, color, religion, sex, sexual orientation, gender identity or expression, age, disability, marital status, citizenship, national origin, genetic information, or any other characteristic protected by law.

If you see or hear or experience something that is unacceptable, please tell your team leader, function leader, the CEO, or the HR, and we promise we will take the necessary actions, keeping you confidentiality in mind. You can also report an incident anonymously using our internal anonymous channel and you can trust on us to act judiciously.

In case an employee is found guilty of violating any of the above aspects, the employment will be terminated with immediate effect and the necessary action will be taken as per the applicable laws.

Please bear in mind that because we work remotely across countries, it can be possible that some local laws conflict. In such cases, we will have to use the law where TDW is headquartered.
